# v0.12.4 Playtest Route Cards

Date: 2026-05-18

Use these cards with `docs/V0124_MANUAL_HUMAN_PLAYTEST_PACKET.md`. Play one route per session when possible. If a route needs a prepared save and you do not have one, write `not tested - no prepared save`.

## Route A - Baseline Cautious Route

Player mindset:

- You are playing normally and cautiously.
- You are not optimizing, speedrunning, or deliberately exploiting knowledge.
- You read objectives and warnings before acting.

What to do:

- Capture objectives in the obvious order.
- Build production before major pushes.
- Retreat or regroup if units are hurt.
- Preserve units when it is reasonable.
- Use hero abilities when the game seems to ask for a push or defense.

What not to do:

- Do not deliberately delay forever.
- Do not rush the enemy base before reading the map.
- Do not ignore objective text just to see what breaks.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Tutorial / Proving Grounds if you need a control warmup.
- Border Village.
- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch.

Watchpoints:

- Does normal objective order feel fair?
- Are warnings noticed without special effort?
- Does victory feel earned rather than automatic?
- Does the result screen explain the outcome?

Questions to answer:

- What was your first clear action in each mission?
- Did you ever feel lost?
- Did you know when to retreat or push?
- Did a warning change what you did?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if cautious play loses before you understand what to do.
- Concern if cautious play wins while ignoring objectives, production, and pressure.
- Pass if mistakes feel readable and recovery feels possible.

## Route B - No-Retinue Route

Player mindset:

- You are checking whether the campaign stays fair without carried veteran power.
- You rely on current-battle production and ordinary campaign rewards.

What to do:

- Avoid deploying or leaning on saved veterans.
- Build and train normally.
- Follow objective guidance.
- Use support nodes only as a normal player would.

What not to do:

- Do not intentionally cripple yourself beyond avoiding retinue reliance.
- Do not skip production and then call the route unfair.
- Do not use a maxed veteran save.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Border Village as baseline.
- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch if unlocked and practical.

Watchpoints:

- Does Ashen Outpost remain beatable without veteran cushion?
- Do Cinderfen pressure warnings give enough reaction time?
- Do losses feel like ordinary attrition or punishment for missing a hidden requirement?

Questions to answer:

- Did no-retinue feel fair but harder, or did it feel mandatory to bring veterans?
- Did you know how to recover from losses?
- Did resources convert into enough army if you acted decisively?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if no-retinue loses repeatedly despite following objectives and converting resources into army.
- Concern if the game never explains why the route is harder.
- Pass if no-retinue is slower and more attritional but understandable.

## Route C - One-Veteran Route

Player mindset:

- You bring one meaningful veteran advantage and protect it.
- You are testing whether one veteran feels earned without making the route automatic.

What to do:

- Use one veteran, usually a meaningful Militia or Ranger.
- Keep the veteran with the main army.
- Build and capture objectives normally.
- Notice whether the veteran changes your confidence.

What not to do:

- Do not stack every possible carryover advantage.
- Do not suicide the veteran as a scout.
- Do not skip core objectives just because the veteran is strong.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch.

Watchpoints:

- Does one veteran help without erasing pressure?
- Is veteran death risk readable?
- Does the route still need production and objective play?

Questions to answer:

- Did the veteran feel valuable?
- Did you understand when the veteran was in danger?
- Would you be upset if the veteran died in that moment?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if one veteran turns every reviewed node into low-attention cleanup.
- Concern if the veteran dies during ordinary play with no readable warning.
- Pass if the veteran feels like earned help and still needs protection.

## Route D - Mixed-Veterans Route

Player mindset:

- You are an experienced player using some carryover, but not trying to max abuse.
- You want the normal "I have played a few battles" campaign path.

What to do:

- Bring a moderate mixed retinue if available.
- Build and train normally.
- Take objectives in a practical order.
- Protect veterans but do not baby them forever.

What not to do:

- Do not add Training Yard II unless you are testing Route E.
- Do not ignore objectives just because your opening army is better.
- Do not treat one clean win as proof that the route is broken.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch.

Watchpoints:

- Does mixed carryover feel like natural campaign growth?
- Does it make non-retinue feel mandatory by comparison?
- Do Cinderfen objectives and pressure still matter?

Questions to answer:

- Did mixed veterans change your plan?
- Were losses still possible?
- Did the route feel satisfying or too effortless?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if mixed veterans repeatedly produce no-loss clears while ignoring map identity.
- Concern if non-retinue feels like a trap after playing this.
- Pass if mixed veterans feel strong but still require decisions.

## Route E - Retinue + Training Yard II Route

Player mindset:

- You are intentionally testing the strongest known profile.
- You want to find out whether earned power becomes too easy.

What to do:

- Use a prepared save with veterans and Training Yard II if available.
- Bring the strongest reasonable active retinue.
- Still build, train, and capture objectives normally.
- Watch whether you actually need those normal actions.

What not to do:

- Do not invent extra advantages outside the current build.
- Do not skip all production on the first run unless a normal run was already trivial.
- Do not call it overpowered only because it feels good.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch.

Watchpoints:

- Does the route flatten map identity?
- Can you ignore shrine, road, pressure, production, or retreat?
- Are veterans ever threatened?
- Does it feel like satisfying earned power or like the game gave up?

Questions to answer:

- What did you still need to think about?
- Were any objectives optional in practice?
- Would you recommend nerfing it after one run, or only watching it?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if multiple testers report no-loss clears with little objective, production, or pressure engagement.
- Concern if Cinderfen warnings stop mattering entirely.
- Pass if it feels powerful but earned and still asks for real decisions.

## Route F - Greedy Economy Route

Player mindset:

- You prioritize resources, value, and side income.
- You are checking whether greed is risky in a fair way.

What to do:

- Take economy/value choices when offered.
- Capture side income.
- Build production, but allow yourself to delay the final push.
- Record when resources pile up and whether you converted them.

What not to do:

- Do not intentionally refuse to build units forever.
- Do not treat "I floated resources" as proof the economy is weak.
- Do not demand that greed be as safe as cautious play.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch.

Watchpoints:

- Do timeouts feel fair and self-inflicted?
- Does the game teach that resources must become army pressure?
- Do pressure warnings feel like useful prompts or just noise?

Questions to answer:

- Did you know when greed had gone too far?
- Did you understand why you won, lost, or timed out?
- Did results explain how to recover next time?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if greed loses before its risk/reward tradeoff is visible.
- Concern if multiple testers cannot diagnose resource-to-army conversion after timeouts.
- Pass if greed feels tempting, risky, and learnable.

## Route G - Fast Army Route

Player mindset:

- You build quickly, attack early, and accept some risk.
- You are checking whether speed rewards decisive play or trivializes Cinderfen.

What to do:

- Prioritize early units and quick objective pressure.
- Attack once you have a plausible force.
- Keep moving instead of waiting for perfect economy.
- Record losses and whether you skipped map beats.

What not to do:

- Do not use every veteran/Training Yard advantage unless combining with Route E intentionally.
- Do not ignore heavy losses just because the timer is fast.
- Do not call fast success bad unless it bypasses meaningful decisions.

Missions/nodes to play:

- Ashen Outpost.
- Cinderfen Crossing.
- Cinderfen Watch.

Watchpoints:

- Does speed trivialize Cinderfen Crossing?
- Does Cinderfen Watch still create pressure and reaction needs?
- Are losses, risk, or missed rewards enough to balance speed?

Questions to answer:

- Did you skip shrine, road, pressure, or enemy-wave identity?
- Did fast play feel exciting or hollow?
- Were you punished for bad aggression in a readable way?

Pass/fail concern triggers:

- Concern if fast play repeatedly clears Cinderfen with low losses and little objective engagement.
- Concern if slower routes feel pointless by comparison.
- Pass if fast play is strong, risky, and fun without erasing the scenario.
