# v0.12.2 Simulator Balance Review

Date: 2026-05-17

Scope: Phase 2 simulator and telemetry review for v0.12.2 Human Balance Watchpoint Review. This review uses the existing `PLAYTEST_TELEMETRY.md` and `PLAYTEST_TELEMETRY.json` generated by the deterministic scripted playtest suite. No simulator reporting changes were needed because the current telemetry already answers the core watchpoint questions at a structural level.

## Source Data

- `PLAYTEST_TELEMETRY.json`
- `PLAYTEST_TELEMETRY.md`
- 255 deterministic runs.
- 85 campaign battle node/profile summaries.
- Scripts: `safe_beginner`, `greedy_economy`, `fast_army`.
- Profiles include no Stronghold, Tier I paths, Quartermaster paths, retinue profiles, Retinue + Training Yard II, Retinue + Quartermaster II, and Waystation Shrine Attunement.

Current analysis summary:

- Structural `too_easy`: none.
- Structural `too_hard`: none.
- Stronghold warnings: none.
- Enemy pressure warnings: none.
- Ashen Outpost beatable: yes.
- Suggested tuning is review-oriented: inspect strategy spread before numeric changes.

Telemetry caveat:

- The simulator records battle result, final army size, units lost, hero level, and hero XP, but it does not independently record hero HP/death as a separate survival metric. v0.12.2 should not infer hero-specific balance from this telemetry alone.

## Watchpoint Decisions From Simulator Evidence

Retinue + Training Yard II:

- Strongest profile, especially in Cinderfen.
- No structural `too_easy` flag.
- Current evidence supports watch/defer, not numeric nerf.

Greedy Economy:

- Risk profile is working: safe first-wave survival, many timeouts, high resource float, high losses after pressure.
- Failure is likely conversion/pacing/readability rather than unfair pressure.
- Current evidence supports no numeric tuning; human notes should check whether results/guidance makes the risk understandable.

Fast Army:

- Fast clears are real in Cinderfen.
- Fast Army also has defeats and first-wave non-survival across the full suite.
- Current evidence supports legitimate speed profile, not immediate slowdown.

Early campaign defeats:

- Safe Beginner wins every run.
- Early Easy nodes have no losses.
- Current evidence supports no early campaign difficulty reduction.

Pressure warning fairness:

- No enemy-pressure warnings in analyzer.
- Pressure-enabled runs show warnings before/with pressure and no structural too-hard flags.
- Current evidence supports fairness, with human review still needed for perceived stress and clarity.
