# v0.88 Barrosan Freeholds Style-Frame Brief

Status: future-generation brief only. No images are generated or approved by this checkpoint.

## Intent

The Barrosan Freeholds are the first polished player-facing faction priority. They should read as resilient human highland villages shaped by granite, wet roads, timber, hearth light, defensive stewardship, and practical craft.

The faction should feel grounded, protective, and worn by weather, not pristine or royal.

## Silhouettes

- Low, sturdy, sheltering profiles.
- Strong rooflines, stone bases, timber braces, tool shapes, cloaks, shields, lanterns.
- Defensive silhouettes should look built by people who survive long winters and raids.
- Workers must be instantly readable at RTS scale through packs, tools, and posture.

## Proportions

- Human proportions remain grounded.
- Armor is practical and layered rather than huge fantasy plate.
- Buildings should feel compact and weight-bearing, with stone lower levels and timber upper framing.
- Command Hall should be wider and more stable than tall.

## Materials

- Dark wet granite.
- Weathered timber.
- Hearth iron.
- Waxed cloth, wool, leather, rope, slate roofs.
- Warm firelight against cool rain/fog.

## Buildings

- Village forms, old roads, defensive walls, hearth windows, tool sheds, shrines, and mine supports.
- Barracks should look like militia mustering space, not a palace armory.
- Shrine should feel ancient and local, not generic temple fantasy.

## Worker Concept

- Strong silhouette: tool bundle, shoulder cloak, lantern or small hammer, practical boots.
- Role: build, repair, site support.
- Must not look like a peasant caricature or modern construction worker.
- Readability anchor: tool angle plus warm lantern/hearth accent.

## Basic Troop

- Militia/frontline.
- Shield or spear/axe profile, layered cloth and leather, short cloak, practical helmet.
- Reads as hold-the-line village defender.

## Ranged Troop

- Ranger/scout archer.
- Leaner silhouette, hood or weather cloak, short bow/crossbow option for future concept review, ridge-path mobility.
- Reads as accurate but fragile.

## Elite Unit

- Hearthguard or Freehold oathbearer concept direction.
- Heavier shield, granite/iron motif, hearth glow accent, defensive stewardship identity.
- No huge paladin glow or royal knight fantasy.

## Hero

- Commander-champion with grounded authority.
- Distinct cloak, oath token, worn weapon, map/standard or lantern motif.
- Should support Warrior/Seer/Commander build identities without locking one class visually.

## Command Hall

- Granite hall with timber frame, hearth windows, banners, covered gathering porch, rain-dark roof.
- Strong RTS silhouette: broad base, central hearth/chimney, clear entrance.

## Effects

- Hearth light, rain glints, hammer sparks, restrained Lume-teal accents only when linked-site/magic context is explicit.
- Avoid particle overload.

## Forbidden Lookalikes

- Warcraft human town hall or footman silhouettes.
- Generic medieval mobile strategy buildings.
- Beige parchment fantasy village.
- Clean royal castle/paladin faction.
- Scottish stereotype costume shorthand without original design work.

## Consistency Risks

- Too many warm tones can erase the wet highland mood.
- Too much granite can make the faction visually dead.
- Worker readability can collapse if cloaks and tools blend together.
- Buildings may drift into generic medieval Europe unless Lume/Salto details are maintained.

## AI-Generation Risks

- Inconsistent tool anatomy and hand counts.
- Incoherent timber/stone construction.
- Overdesigned armor at RTS scale.
- Random heraldry that resembles protected IP.
- Lantern/hearth accents turning into generic magical glow.

## Human-Review Questions

- Does the Worker read correctly at small scale?
- Does the faction feel like defensive stewards rather than generic knights?
- Are granite, timber, wet roads, and hearth light all present without clutter?
- Does any silhouette resemble a protected RTS property too closely?
- Does the design leave enough room for later Lume identity?
