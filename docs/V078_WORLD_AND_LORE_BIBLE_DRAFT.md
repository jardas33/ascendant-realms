# v0.78 World And Lore Bible Draft

Date: 2026-05-30

## Status

This is a draft identity bible for human review. It does not change runtime copy, IDs, maps, assets, or campaign data.

## Core World Direction

The world is deeply inspired by the atmosphere of Barroso and the northern highlands without literally reproducing Portugal. It should evoke granite villages, mountain valleys, fog, rain, wet roads, old paths, mines, springs, shrines, standing stones, ruined fortresses, communal loyalty, long memory, wolves, winter rituals, hearth fire, blackened timber, and a harsh but beautiful frontier.

The opening village is proposed as `Salto`.

The wider frontier region is proposed as `The Barrosan Marches`.

The living power beneath the land is proposed as `Lume`.

## Lume

Lume is not generic mana. It is part fire, part memory, part life-force, and part territorial bond. It flows beneath mines, springs, shrines, standing stones, old roads, ruins, caves, and mountains.

Lume can be awakened, guided, and defended. It cannot be treated as a consequence-free fuel source.

## The First Oath

Long ago, the first Jardas forged an oath with the land:

- Lume could be awakened.
- Lume could be guided.
- Lume could be defended.
- Lume could never be treated as a mere resource without consequence.

That oath has been broken. The central conflict is not simply who controls Lume, but what kind of relationship with Lume can survive.

## The Jardas

A Jardas is an oath-bound hero capable of awakening and binding Lume sites. The Jardas is neither a landlord nor a miner-king. The role is guardian, negotiator, commander, and living witness.

The protagonist begins in Salto as a newly awakened Jardas. Their growth should connect hero progression, site control, Retinue continuity, and campaign choices.

## Salto

Salto is the emotional home of the campaign:

- a granite highland village,
- small enough to feel personal,
- old enough to carry memory,
- threatened enough to justify command,
- resilient enough to become a stronghold later.

Salto should eventually feel like a place worth defending, not just a menu between battles.

## Captain Malrec

Captain Malrec remains the Act 1 rival commander. In the lore draft, he is intelligent, charismatic, dangerous, and partially understandable. He believes control over Lume is necessary to prevent destruction. He should not read as a disposable villain.

His conflict with the protagonist should ask a real question: is Lume safer under disciplined control, communal oath, hidden stewardship, or something more radical?

## Playable Races And Lume

Each playable race needs a distinct moral, cultural, strategic, and practical relationship with Lume. No race should be simplistically good or evil.

Examples:

- Barrosan Freeholds defend Lume through communal stewardship.
- Ashen Covenant controls Lume through disciplined overuse and sacrifice.
- Moura Court bargains with hidden Lume currents and broken promises.
- Granitborn remember Lume as stone-deep memory.
- Wolfveil Clans sense Lume through territory, pack movement, and living paths.
- Careto Host disrupts Lume through ritual tempo and winter masks.
- Rootbound Concord grows through Lume as an ecosystem.
- Deepbell League engineers Lume through mines, resonance, and machinery.

## Proposed Future Act 1 Reframe

Runtime copy is unchanged in v0.78, but the future narrative framing should be:

`Act 1 - Ashes over Salto`

Purpose: introduce Salto, awaken the protagonist as a Jardas, teach mines, Workers, armies, sites, Retinue, rival pressure, and Captain Malrec, then reveal that the local raid is part of a much larger Lume conflict.
