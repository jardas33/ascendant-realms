# v0.7.3 Manual Cinderfen Pressure Playtest Checklist

Status: Phase 6 human checklist. This is for a player using the game normally. Do not inspect code, test hooks, telemetry, or developer tools while following it.

## 1. How To Start

Open Ascendant Realms and use an existing campaign save that can reach Chapter 2 Cinderfen, or ask for a seeded preview build if you do not have one.

Play with normal mouse and keyboard pacing. Do not rush only because this is a checklist. The useful part is what you notice while playing naturally.

After each battle, write a few notes while the feel is fresh.

## 2. What To Play

Play these battles:

1. Cinderfen Crossing
2. Cinderfen Watch

If you have time, repeat one of them with a different style:

- Safe Beginner: stabilize, claim income, build a modest force, then push.
- Greedy Economy: claim income and delay the final push while building up.
- Fast Army: build quickly and try to finish fast.
- Retinue + Training Yard II: use saved veterans and Stronghold power if your save has them.

## 3. Cinderfen Crossing Notes

Watch for:

- Did you notice the Cinder Shrine before fighting deep into the map?
- Did you notice `Cinder Shrine Surge` when you captured it?
- Did you notice the enemy pressure warning after the shrine?
- Did the warning appear early enough to matter?
- Did the warning tell you what to do next?
- Did the shrine route feel worth caring about?
- If you cleared fast, did skipping most pressure feel fun, clever, or too cheap?

Write down:

- Route taken.
- Units you relied on.
- Whether the warning changed your next order.
- Whether the battle felt better, worse, or unchanged compared with before.

## 4. Cinderfen Watch Notes

Watch for:

- Did Watch Road feel like the obvious first objective?
- Did the Watch Captain warning feel fair or too early?
- Did you notice the later raised-road warning?
- Did the warning make you guard income, build defense, regroup, or push faster?
- Did ordinary battle messages make the warning hard to read?
- If you played greedy, did the timeout or late pressure feel understandable?
- If you played fast, did pressure add tension or feel irrelevant?

Write down:

- Route taken.
- Units you relied on.
- Whether Watch Road felt rewarding.
- Whether the pressure warning changed your plan.
- Whether the battle felt fair.

## 5. Ratings

After each battle, rate these from 1 to 5:

| Rating | 1 Means | 5 Means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Warning clarity | I did not understand it | I immediately understood it |
| Warning timing | It was uselessly early or late | It appeared at the right moment |
| Pressure fairness | It felt unfair or random | It felt like fair enemy response |
| Strategic usefulness | It did not affect my thinking | It helped me make a decision |
| Fun | It made the battle worse | It made the battle better |
| Frustration | I felt no frustration | I felt very frustrated |

Use whole numbers. A 3 is useful: it means "fine, but not special."

## 6. What To Send Back

Please send:

- Which battle you played.
- Which style you used: Safe Beginner, Greedy Economy, Fast Army, Retinue + Training Yard II, or your own style.
- Your 1 to 5 ratings.
- A short note on whether you noticed the pressure warning.
- A short note on whether the warning changed what you did.
- A screenshot if something looked confusing, crowded, or especially clear.
- Whether the battle felt better, worse, or unchanged.

Useful examples:

- "Crossing, Fast Army. I ignored the shrine and won quickly. It felt clever, not cheap. Warning clarity N/A because I never saw one."
- "Watch, Greedy Economy. Warning clarity 4, timing 4, fairness 3, usefulness 3, fun 3, frustration 4. I understood the road warning but still floated resources and timed out."
- "Crossing, Safe Beginner. I noticed Cinder Shrine Surge but missed the later warning while fighting. Timing 3, clarity 4."

## 7. Strict Boundary

This checklist is only for feel.

Do not inspect code, logs, telemetry, tests, save files, or internal debug state. Play normally and report what you noticed.

Do not try to prove a balance theory in one run. A surprising feeling is enough to report.
