# v0.88 Ashen Covenant Style-Frame Brief

Status: future-generation brief only. No images are generated or approved by this checkpoint.

## Intent

The Ashen Covenant is the first antagonist contrast for the Barrosan Freeholds. It should read as a disciplined altered civilization using blackened iron, controlled flame, smoke, chains, overcharge, and siege pressure.

It must not become generic demons, orcs, undead, or random lava armor.

## Silhouettes

- Angular, drilled, compressed, and dangerous.
- Blackened iron ribs, chain lines, ember vents, smoke banners, heavy masks or helms.
- Squads should feel organized and coercive, not chaotic.

## Proportions

- Mostly human or altered-human civilization, with controlled distortion.
- Heavier shoulders/helms and narrow waists can imply discipline and constraint.
- Elite silhouettes may include overcharge vents or chained armor, but must remain readable.

## Materials

- Blackened iron.
- Charred timber.
- Ember-lit seams.
- Sooted cloth.
- Chain, rivet, dark leather, ash-gray smoke.
- Flame is disciplined and directional, not decorative everywhere.

## Buildings

- Fortified foundry-like structures, smoke stacks, chain gates, ember furnaces, angular walls.
- Defensive posture should feel engineered.
- Site-control structures may show extraction and pressure rather than stewardship.

## Worker Concept

- Ashen laborer/forgebound support figure for future antagonist identity.
- Tool silhouette and chain/iron harness.
- Must avoid dehumanizing into generic monster worker unless lore approves.

## Basic Troop

- Disciplined infantry.
- Shield/iron blade, ash mask, ember seam, compact marching silhouette.
- Reads as steady pressure.

## Ranged Troop

- Crossbow, flame-bolt, or ember lancer concept direction.
- Must read as ranged at RTS scale through weapon line and stance.
- Flame effect stays compact.

## Elite Unit

- Overcharged ironbound squad or covenant enforcer.
- Modest visual escalation: stronger helm, ember vents, heavier chain/iron silhouette.
- No giant boss creature.

## Hero

- Rival commander/ashen captain.
- Controlled command silhouette, smoke cloak or chain banner, visible authority.
- Should support doctrine/elites without needing a new boss system.

## Command Hall

- Blackened command forge or bastion.
- Broad defensive mass, vents, smoke, chain gate, ember core.
- Must contrast with Barrosan hearth warmth by feeling coercive and industrial.

## Effects

- Ember sparks, smoke plumes, overcharge pulses, chain-tension marks.
- Ashen fire colors signal hostile pressure and overcharge.
- Avoid turning every attack into a large flame burst.

## Forbidden Lookalikes

- Warcraft orc/undead/human siege motifs.
- Generic demon fortress.
- Warhammer-style chaos armor.
- Mobile-game lava faction.
- Pure black silhouettes with no readable forms.

## Consistency Risks

- Too much flame creates global visual inflation.
- Smoke can obscure unit readability.
- Black iron can crush contrast if not rim-lit carefully.
- Altered-human cues may drift into monster faction too early.

## AI-Generation Risks

- Random skulls, horns, demonic faces, or copied chaos iconography.
- Incoherent chain routing and armor joints.
- Excessive cinematic smoke that hides gameplay silhouettes.
- Flame colors leaking into neutral UI states.

## Human-Review Questions

- Does the faction read as disciplined altered civilization?
- Is the flame controlled and meaningful?
- Can basic, ranged, elite, and hero silhouettes be distinguished quickly?
- Does anything look like a generic demon/orc/undead faction?
- Can the design support current enemy doctrines without suggesting new mechanics?
