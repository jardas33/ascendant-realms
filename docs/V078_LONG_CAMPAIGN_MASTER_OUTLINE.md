# v0.78 Long Campaign Master Outline

Date: 2026-05-30

## Status

This is a future campaign direction draft. v0.78 does not add missions, maps, copy migrations, art, or runtime campaign data.

## Act 1 - Ashes over Salto

- Narrative purpose: introduce Salto, awaken the protagonist as a Jardas, teach mines, Workers, armies, sites, Retinue, and rival pressure.
- Locations: Salto, nearby mines, shrine roads, Ashen Outpost.
- Races introduced: Barrosan Freeholds, Ashen Covenant.
- Mission variety: tutorial, skirmish, base building, resource control, rival finale.
- Rival development: Captain Malrec becomes an intelligent pressure point, not a disposable villain.
- Stronghold progression: Salto begins as home and defense hub.
- Lume revelations: Lume is living memory and territorial bond.
- Retinue opportunities: early survivor continuity and recovery.
- Optional hunts: local threats only.
- Player choices: protect sites, preserve resources, answer Malrec's pressure.
- Consequences: Act 1 complete, replay optional objectives remain.
- Reusable foundations: current Act 1 loop, Ashen Outpost finale, Retinue, relics, skills, campaign rewards.
- Deferred systems: runtime reframe, Salto visual stronghold, branching story.
- Must not implement prematurely: Act 2 expansion without human review.

## Act 2 - The Springs Beneath Stone

- Narrative purpose: explore deeper Lume lore, buried palaces, springs, caves, and Moura mysteries.
- Locations: hidden springs, caves, underground courts, old roads.
- Races introduced: Moura Court, Deepbell League as possible mine specialists.
- Mission variety: hidden routes, shrine defense, cave assaults, elite skirmishes.
- Rival development: Malrec's argument becomes more complicated; new rivals may reveal non-Ashen motives.
- Stronghold progression: Salto gains spring/road connections.
- Lume revelations: Lume can remember promises and betrayals.
- Retinue opportunities: scouts, guides, wounded survivor choices.
- Optional hunts: cave beasts, spring guardians.
- Player choices: bargain, reveal, preserve, or exploit hidden sites.
- Consequences: alliances and trust affect later site access.
- Reusable foundations: site control, tactical plans, dynamic objectives.
- Deferred systems: hidden-route engine, Moura art, new faction runtime.
- Must not implement prematurely: illusion mechanics without readability tests.

## Act 3 - Winter Masks

- Narrative purpose: enter wild high ridges, masks, pack societies, and unstable alliances.
- Locations: high passes, winter villages, ridge paths, ritual grounds.
- Races introduced: Careto Host, Wolfveil Clans.
- Mission variety: raids, defense under pressure, ritual disruptions, hunts.
- Rival development: rivals respond to the player's treatment of Lume and Retinue.
- Stronghold progression: Salto prepares for winter and refugees.
- Lume revelations: Lume can pulse through season, fear, and territory.
- Retinue opportunities: rescue, scars, loyalty tests.
- Optional hunts: ridge monsters, winter spirits.
- Player choices: honor rituals, break them, ally with packs, or defend roads.
- Consequences: road safety, pack trust, ritual backlash.
- Reusable foundations: doctrines, events, Patrol/control groups, Retinue recovery.
- Deferred systems: ritual tempo, beast animation, morale systems.
- Must not implement prematurely: broad chaos mechanics without player-readable counters.

## Act 4 - The War of Eight Banners

- Narrative purpose: open the world fully and reveal competing racial visions for Lume.
- Locations: Marches, fortresses, rootlands, mines, roads, border strongholds.
- Races introduced: Rootbound Concord and Granitborn fully enter, with all eight banners visible.
- Mission variety: large assaults, multi-front defense, diplomacy consequences, stronghold attacks.
- Rival development: multiple commanders adapt to the player's methods.
- Stronghold progression: Salto becomes a lived-in strategic home.
- Lume revelations: no faction has a complete moral claim.
- Retinue opportunities: named veterans, losses, loyalty, memorials.
- Optional hunts: corrupted sites, ancient guardians.
- Player choices: alliance, betrayal, preservation, overcharge, mercy.
- Consequences: faction trust, site networks, late-game army options.
- Reusable foundations: campaign nodes, optional objectives, rewards, enemy doctrines.
- Deferred systems: faction diplomacy, multiple playable races, large art pipeline.
- Must not implement prematurely: all-race roster before one polished vertical slice.

## Act 5 - The Heart Below Barroso

- Narrative purpose: descend toward the oldest Lume vein and resolve the meaning of the Jardas oath.
- Locations: deep mines, oldest shrines, mountain heart, broken oath sites.
- Races introduced: no new core races; focus on consequences and final alignments.
- Mission variety: finale chains, high-level hunts, fortress breaches, hero-led decisions.
- Rival development: Malrec and other rivals may return altered by choices.
- Stronghold progression: Salto reflects the final campaign state.
- Lume revelations: origin of Jardas and the first oath.
- Retinue opportunities: final survivor stories and sacrifices.
- Optional hunts: ancient Lume monsters and corrupted fortresses.
- Player choices: restore, reform, dominate, release, or earn another outcome.
- Consequences: ending states for Salto, Lume, allies, rivals, and Retinue.
- Reusable foundations: finale phases, Results debriefs, Retinue, hero builds.
- Deferred systems: ending-state persistence, cinematic presentation, high-level bosses.
- Must not implement prematurely: final-choice logic before earlier acts are human-approved.
