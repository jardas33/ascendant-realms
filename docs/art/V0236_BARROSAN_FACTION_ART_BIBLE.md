# v0.236 Barrosan / Human Faction Art Bible

Status: production-direction foundation for the Salto battlefield slice.

This document defines the visual contract for Barrosan buildings and settlement terrain. It is intentionally narrower than a complete game-wide art guide: it establishes one coherent Human faction standard that future races must contrast against.

## 1. Faction identity

The Barrosans are a Human frontier culture building permanent footholds in dangerous country. Their settlements should feel rural, practical and fortified rather than courtly or ornamental.

Core identity:

- layered fieldstone foundations that visibly carry the weight of every building;
- limewashed and warm plaster walls repaired in locally available shades;
- exposed timber framing, braces, lintels and practical exterior work structures;
- aged clay and terracotta roofs with dark timber eaves;
- defensible entrances, raised civic thresholds and working yards;
- restrained ochre, iron-red and cream heraldry;
- Lume crystal used as a scarce industrial-mystic resource, never as generic neon decoration.

The faction is not:

- a generic medieval toy village;
- a collection of identical cottages;
- pristine high-fantasy palace architecture;
- science-fiction machinery;
- a floating kit catalogue disconnected from roads, yards and terrain.

The intended read is: **a warm, inhabited frontier settlement that expects trouble and knows how to endure it.**

## 2. Shape language

All silhouettes must survive an isometric RTS camera. Large role shapes come first; trim and props clarify the read without eroding it.

### Keep / base

- Tallest and most vertical Barrosan landmark.
- Broad layered stone base, defended entry and an unmistakable central civic axis.
- Main roof and tower caps form a crown above the settlement.
- Steps, banners, gate trim and ordered stone courses communicate authority.
- Avoid a single unbroken box or a palace-like abundance of delicate spires.

### Barracks / workshop

- Long, low and practical.
- Dominant pitched roof with visible timber rhythm.
- Attached workshop mass, chimney and open work-yard edge.
- Weapon racks, training posts, crates, logs and tools identify the military-production role.
- Clutter should create one legible yard composition, not random scatter.

### Mine / Lume works

- Lower, heavier industrial silhouette.
- Dark roof and deep portal framed by substantial timber supports.
- Extraction frame, pulley, stone piles and crystal shards radiate from the work face.
- Cyan Lume is concentrated at the well and extracted shards; it should not wash the whole building.
- The shape must combine quarry weight with controlled arcane energy.

### Bridge and roads

- Infrastructure is built into the ground.
- Bridge abutments land on shaped banks, with timber braces and visible deck rhythm.
- Roads widen at entries and yards, narrow between destinations and show worn centers.
- No road should read as a bright rectangular panel hovering above grass.

### Houses and future economy buildings

- Smaller, warmer and more domestic than military landmarks.
- Lower rooflines, compact yards, lean-tos, storage and working-garden cues.
- Retain stone bases, limewash, timber braces and clay roofs so they remain Barrosan.
- Their silhouettes should support the keep rather than compete with it.

## 3. Roof language

Every normal pitched roof must have:

- one highest central ridge;
- two planes descending from that ridge to opposite eaves;
- eaves that visibly overhang the wall footprint;
- separate ridge cap or ridge beam;
- dark fascia and eave undersides;
- role-appropriate clay, aged terracotta or dark timber coloration;
- restrained weather variation that follows roof construction.

Flat roofs are allowed only when a building role specifically demands a working platform, defensive deck or small lean-to. They must not be used as toy-like lids.

Forbidden:

- inverted or center-collapsed roofs;
- folded planes;
- unsupported roof slabs;
- saturated uniform orange roofs;
- decorative roof noise that destroys the building silhouette.

- Terrain remains quieter than buildings so RTS role silhouettes stay readable.

## 5. Detail rules

Every major building requires:

- a layered foundation or visible wall-base course;
- at least one dark contact or recessed entrance zone;
- at least two timber shades where timber is a primary material;
- at least one controlled plaster or stone shade break on a broad facade;
- separate roof ridge, fascia and dark eave treatment;
- entrance emphasis through steps, lintel, canopy, banner or yard wear;
- a minimum of three role-specific exterior detail groups.

Role-specific groups:

- Keep: formal steps, paired banners, gate framing, ordered stone courses, civic marker.
- Barracks/workshop: weapon rack, training post, tool cart or bench, crate/log storage.
- Mine/Lume: extraction support, pulley, stone pile, crystal shards, reinforced portal.

Clutter rules:

- Cluster props around work zones and entrances.
- Keep the main road, bridge and tactical reading lanes clear.
- Vary orientation and scale modestly.
- Do not use random scatter as a substitute for composition.
- Every prop should explain how the settlement works.

## 6. Terrain rules

- The battlefield must read as one continuous landform, not a board assembled from square tiles.
- Use irregular silhouettes, overlapping organic patches and varied edge lengths.
- Grass/dirt variation should be broad enough to read at RTS distance and subtle enough not to compete with units.
- Building yards may be firmer and more geometric near foundations, but their outer edges must dissolve into terrain.
- Roads use curved or angled ribbons with variable width, a darker embedded bed and a worn center.
- Road junctions widen naturally; approaches align with doors and bridge landings.
- River water sits below the surrounding ground.
- Banks must have shaped edges, darker depth material and intermittent stone/moss breakup.
- Bridge abutments must visibly meet both banks.
- Avoid repeated evenly spaced stones along every edge.

## 7. Lighting and camera

- Use an orthographic isometric RTS camera for the primary verdict.
- Preserve one full-field overview and several close role reads.
- Warm key light may reinforce clay and timber, with restrained cool fill for form separation.
- Contact shadows are important; crushed blacks are not.
- Do not use extreme close-ups, depth-of-field or cinematic angles to hide gameplay readability.
- Material variation must remain visible from the overview, not only in beauty shots.

## 8. Future-race separation

Barrosan architecture is the warm, practical, human baseline. Future factions must diverge structurally, not merely by recoloring it.

- **Ashen:** harsher angular masses, charred surfaces, volcanic stone, exposed heat and hostile asymmetry.
- **Sylvan:** organic curves, living wood, root and canopy structures, soft boundaries and grown rather than assembled forms.
- **Stone / dwarf-like:** massive carved geometry, compressed proportions, deep portals, underground or fortress logic.
- **Arcane:** precise vertical geometry, luminous structure, suspended elements and controlled impossible balance.

If a future faction can be made by swapping the Barrosan roof color, the faction design has failed.

## 9. v0.236 implementation contract

The production-direction slice must:

- preserve all corrected v0.235 ridge-to-eave roof geometry;
- upgrade exactly three existing building modules: keep, barracks/workshop and mine/Lume;
- use a new versioned Blender source and GLB rather than overwriting v0.235;
- add role-specific prop modules and place them as composed yard groups;
- replace visible square terrain-cell seams with continuous irregular terrain;
- replace panel-like roads and river banks with shaped ribbons and organic patches;
- preserve the v0.234/v0.235 connected battlefield layout and isometric readability;
- change no gameplay, save, economy, selection, pathing, command, minimap, objective, production or launcher-default behavior.

## 10. v0.237 material-richness and inhabited-detail addendum

This addendum raises the finish standard without changing the underlying Barrosan identity or the corrected ridge-to-eave roof contract.

### Barrosan material-detail rules

- Roof variation follows construction: restrained tile-row or ridge-strip rhythm, dark eaves and occasional aged bands. It must never become random confetti across the silhouette.
- Lime plaster uses broad shade changes, repair patches, hairline cracks and contact staining. A wall should read first as one wall and second as a repaired frontier surface.
- Stone foundations show courses, chipped edges, moss at damp contacts and darker grounding beneath walls, steps and bridge supports.
- Timber uses at least two related values plus selective pegs, beam caps or iron brackets where joints need explanation.
- Contact dirt belongs beneath eaves, walls, thresholds, props and supports. It is a grounding device, not a global black outline.
- Lume crystals remain a controlled cyan accent. Emission must reveal the extraction role without flooding nearby materials or competing with objective readability.

### Vegetation vocabulary

- Small round and wind-shaped bushes provide low silhouette breakup.
- Grass clumps appear in composed groups along road edges, yards and quieter terrain pockets.
- Reeds and bank plants belong near water and damp soil, with moss or low ground cover near stone and river edges.
- Trees use at least two distinct size and crown shapes: young narrow frontier growth and broader mature shelter trees.
- Vegetation remains temperate, practical and slightly rugged. Barrosan territory is a worked frontier, not a fantasy jungle.

### Ground-wear rules

- Road centers may show restrained compacted wear, while edges blend through grass islands, dirt scars and trampled patches.
- Building yards receive role-specific wear: drill and cart traffic at the barracks, formal threshold wear at the keep and ore/cart scars at the mine.
- Shallow terrace cues may break broad terrain planes, but they must not imply blocked routes or change gameplay collision.
- Island and river edges use irregular silhouettes, intermittent stone, reeds and moss instead of continuous decorative borders.

### Avoiding over-noise in the RTS view

- Cluster details around landmarks, thresholds, working yards and damp edges; preserve open breathing room between those clusters.
- Prefer a few readable families repeated with variation over many unrelated tiny props.
- The three landmarks, road network, bridge and river must remain legible before individual details are noticed.
- If a detail improves only a close-up but muddies the full overview, remove or simplify it.

## 11. v0.238 building-roster expansion rules

### Domestic buildings

- Houses are the smallest warm inhabited structures: compact limewashed walls, clay roofs, porches, gardens and household storage.
- Domestic silhouettes stay low and welcoming. They must not inherit barracks-length footprints, weapon-yard cues or defensive massing.
- Doors, porches and gardens face a lane or shared yard so homes read as part of a settlement rather than isolated props.

### Economy buildings

- Food buildings use barn or granary massing, raised storage, broad doors, grain sacks, barrels, hay and field-edge cues.
- Lumber buildings use open working bays, rough timber frames, log/plank storage and saw benches. Their yards are civilian and workmanlike, not drill grounds.
- Markets and depots use open counters, awnings, stacked goods and visible drop-off space. Their silhouette must communicate exchange and logistics before cloth color is noticed.
- Economy buildings may be broader than houses but remain lower and less authoritative than the keep.

### Defense and support buildings

- Forges use dark stone, substantial chimneys, practical metalwork props and one controlled hot accent. They remain craft buildings, not magical laboratories.
- Watchtowers use a stone base and timber lookout logic, but remain clearly smaller and less monumental than the keep.
- Support buildings inherit Barrosan limewash, fieldstone, exposed timber and terracotta while gaining role-specific openings, roofs, yards and props.

### Coherent future expansion

- New buildings begin with a distinct role silhouette and footprint; palette variation is supporting evidence, never the primary distinction.
- Every building needs a grounded foundation, road-facing threshold, correct ridge-to-eave roof, at least two major-surface values and one composed role-prop cluster.
- Reuse the Barrosan material families and construction grammar, while varying roof height, footprint, open/closed bays, chimney, porch, platform and yard organization.
- Avoid catalogue sprawl: future modules should be reviewed together in connected settlement layouts at the real RTS overview.

### Reserved landmark language

- The keep alone owns the highest civic mass, formal defensive crown and strongest paired-banner authority.
- The barracks/workshop alone owns the primary military training and weapons-yard read.
- The mine/Lume site alone owns the dominant extraction portal and large controlled crystal language.
- Domestic, economy and support modules may echo these materials, but must not borrow their landmark scale or signature role cues.

## 12. v0.239 role-silhouette differentiation rules

### Read the role before the palette

- A Barrosan building must communicate its job through body mass, height, roof proportion, open or closed frontage, and yard footprint before material color is considered.
- House means the smallest calm closed body, low porch, domestic footpath, garden, bench, laundry or low fence.
- Granary means raised storage mass, broad loading threshold, loft or vent, food platform, sacks, hay and cart access.
- Lumber means a small service body attached to a larger open timber yard with canopy, A-frame, saw bench, logs and plank stock.
- Forge means a compact heavy stone body, dominant soot-dark chimney, forge mouth, anvil, tool rack and coal or ore pile.
- Watchtower means a narrow vertical shaft, projecting lookout deck, braces, ladder and restrained guard banner; it must never read as a house lifted onto a plinth.
- Market means an open public counter or hall, broad cloth awning, visible goods, loading space and restrained red or ochre trade accents.

### Avoid samey orange-roof house syndrome

- Do not begin every role from the same rectangular body and add props afterward.
- Vary footprint aspect ratio, body-to-yard ratio, ridge height, roof pitch and span, attached canopy direction, vertical projection, foundation weight and threshold width.
- Terracotta remains the faction roof family, but role recognition must survive a grayscale silhouette check.
- Repeated roof color is acceptable only when the body, roof proportion and yard organization are clearly different.

### Future roster variation

- Choose one primary silhouette verb for every future module: compact, raised, open, heavy, vertical, broad, stepped or clustered.
- Give every role one reserved yard language and two supporting props; do not scatter generic crates as a substitute for purpose.
- Compose future buildings in a real settlement overview with roads, vegetation and neighboring structures before approval.
- Preserve clear thresholds, worn working ground and clustered inhabited detail without narrowing tactical routes.

### Landmark reservations remain

- The keep retains the tallest formal civic crown, paired defensive towers and strongest banner authority.
- The barracks/workshop retains the largest military frontage, drill or weapons-yard language and primary troop-production read.
- The mine/Lume landmark retains the dominant extraction portal, cyan crystal concentration and industrial-magical identity.
- Smaller roster buildings may share Barrosan construction materials but may not copy those three landmark signatures.
