# v0.230 Retained Authored Structure-Art Fidelity Report

Date: 2026-06-18

## Decision

`PASS_V0230_RETAINED_AUTHORED_STRUCTURE_ART_FIDELITY`

The isolated v0.230 path materially improves the v0.229 structure blockout without reopening terrain, UI or gameplay scope. The player keep, barracks, central mine/Lume site, ford support and bridge supports now use deterministic authored Godot geometry with true pitched roof prisms, stepped foundations, recessed entrances, asymmetric masses, timber framing and diagonal braces.

## Visual Result

- The keep reads as a fortified hall with unequal towers, a true main gable, porch, braces, buttresses, chimney, banner and stepped entry.
- The barracks reads as a long military hall with a cross-gabled armory annex, gate hierarchy, framed frontage, weapon rack and retained repair-state detail.
- The mine/Lume site reads as a terraced extraction landmark with a dark mine mouth, canopy, workshop roof, A-frame gantry, winch, cart and faceted Lume crystal.
- Bridge-adjacent masonry piers, caps, buttresses and timber knees make the crossing feel supported rather than placed over the river.
- The v0.229 comparator and the v0.228 terrain, road, river and bank foundation remain intact.

## Art Boundary

No generated, downloaded or externally sourced image was added. No derivative or runtime-art slot was created. The result uses deterministic Godot meshes and cached materials only.

## Candid Limitation

This passes as authored low-poly structure art, not final high-detail environment art. Silhouette, hierarchy and identity are materially stronger than v0.229, but wall and roof material richness remains below the supplied fantasy RTS benchmark. The small ford support remains the weakest landmark because its footprint limits silhouette complexity.
