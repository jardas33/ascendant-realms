# v0.88 Wolfveil Clans Silhouette Brief

Status: future-generation brief only. No images are generated or approved by this checkpoint.

## Intent

The Wolfveil Clans are the first strongly non-human silhouette study. They should read as lupine beastkin with pack structure, mist, speed, ridge paths, and mobile warfare.

The core visual problem is preventing human-reskin drift. They must not look like humans in wolf masks.

## Silhouettes

- Forward-leaning, fast, ridge-running forms.
- Lupine heads, neck posture, digitigrade or semi-digitigrade leg language where readable, claws/paws, tails if composition allows.
- Pack hierarchy through stance and gear, not just size.
- Mist/ridge shapes can frame the faction but should not hide anatomy.

## Proportions

- Longer limbs and lower center of gravity than humans.
- Torso and shoulder anatomy should support lupine motion.
- Hands must be functional for weapons/tools without becoming human hands pasted onto wolf heads.
- Avoid extreme monster proportions for basic units.

## Materials

- Hide, layered cloth, bone/stone/timber accents, mist-wet leather, ridge-path travel gear.
- Metal exists but is lighter than Barrosan or Ashen identity.
- Colors lean cool mist, dark fur, ridge stone, muted clan markings.

## Buildings

- Mobile or ridge-adapted clan structures, hide/timber frames, stone cairns, watch posts, mist-lanterns.
- Structures should imply movement and terrain mastery, not fixed fortress identity.

## Worker Concept

- Pack utility unit for future concept.
- Carries tools or bundles in a way compatible with non-human posture.
- Must clearly support build/repair/site-like roles if ever implemented, without copying the Barrosan Worker.

## Basic Troop

- Fast melee scout/fighter.
- Spear, hooked blade, or claw-supported weapon silhouette.
- Reads as mobile pack fighter, not heavily armored frontline.

## Ranged Troop

- Javelin, short bow, sling, or thrown weapon concept direction.
- Side-on stance should preserve lupine anatomy.
- Reads as skirmisher.

## Elite Unit

- Pack champion or mist-runner.
- Stronger crest, cloak, ridge trophies, faster silhouette.
- Avoid giant werewolf brute unless a future design explicitly needs it.

## Hero

- Clan leader, hunter-seer, or pack oath figure.
- Should show intelligence, command, and non-human anatomy.
- Must not look like a human ranger wearing a wolf hood.

## Command Hall

- Clan gathering hall, ridge den, or cairn-lodge.
- Clear faction shape: sloped roofs, hide banners, stone markers, mist openings, pack-circle layout.

## Effects

- Mist trails, speed marks, low dust, ridge wind, subdued Lume interaction if approved later.
- Effects should emphasize movement and pack coordination without adding new runtime mechanics.

## Forbidden Lookalikes

- Werewolf monster mobs with no culture.
- Humans with wolf helmets.
- Warcraft worgen-like silhouettes.
- Generic furry/anime beastfolk styling.
- Random tribal stereotype shorthand.

## Consistency Risks

- Anatomy inconsistency between units.
- Over-human hands, feet, and posture.
- Too much mist hiding silhouettes.
- Cultural props drifting into caricature.

## AI-Generation Risks

- Human-reskin drift.
- Wrong limb counts or broken digitigrade joints.
- Human boots on paw feet.
- Inconsistent muzzle length and ear placement.
- Accidental resemblance to protected lupine fantasy races.

## Human-Review Questions

- Does the silhouette read non-human at thumbnail size?
- Does the design show culture and pack structure, not just monsters?
- Are basic, ranged, elite, and hero roles readable?
- Is anatomy consistent enough for future animation?
- Does any image drift toward a known protected wolf-beast race?
